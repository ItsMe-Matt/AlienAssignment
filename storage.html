Local Storage

What is localStorage?

LocalStorage is a way of storing data in your browser. The interesting part is that data in the localStorage cannot be deleted until its manually deleted via javascript. It will always be present in the browser. (Days, Months, Years)

How do you access localStorage?

When you rightclick on our mouse, you will see the "inspect" button. When that is pressed, on the navbar is a button called "application". When the application button is pressed there is a section on the bottom right called "storage". In the storage category you will find the "Local Storage". In the local storage, you will see a split screen with a "Key" screen on your left, and "Value" screen on your right.

How do you remove the data in localStorage?

There are 2 ways to remove localStorage:

1. type "localStorage.clear()"
2. type "localStorage.removeItem("username")"

What is the history of localStorage?

Before localStorage was introduced to browsers, they were using "cookies". Since localStorage is fairly new, some browsers may not support it.

Extra facts/notes

In the "Key" screen is where you will find the variable you put, and in the "Value" screen is where you will find the value

You are only allowed to store numbers, strings, booleans. But, you cannot store objects or rays.

The localStorage is not designed to store a lot of data, it is mainly used to store a small portion of data.

Some uses for localStorage:

Storing usernames
Storing highscores (Game)
